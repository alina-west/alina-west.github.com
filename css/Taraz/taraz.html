Taraz-City of history

Introduction

Taraz is a city and the administrative center of Jambyl Region in Kazakhstan, located on the Talas River in the south of the country near the border with Kyrgyzstan. It had a population of 330,100 as of the 1999 census, up 9% from 1989, making it one of the fastest-growing cities in the country

History

Taraz is standing on the site of ancient city of Taraz, which flourished as a stop along Silk Road from Europe to China until it was destroyed by Mongol armies in the 13th century. A new town called Aulie-Ata was established on the site by the emirs of Kokand in the late 18th century. The fort and town were captured by Russians in 1864, and in 1938 the town was renamed after Kazakh poet Jam-bul Jabayev (Russian: Zhambyl Dzhabayev; 1846-1945). Phosphorite ore which was found in nearest mountain Karatau predetermined development of phosphorite industry in the region. 3 chemical plants were constructed in the city at once. The collapse of USSR destroyed all the industrial ties and these plants were in heavy crisis. Unemployment was high in the city. Recently all the plants have been almost restored. On January 8th, 1997, by Kazakhstan President's decree the city Zhambyl was renamed into Taraz. In autumn 2002 Taraz celebrated its 2,000th anniversary. Present day Taraz city has large phosphate-processing works, a large power station, sugar, leather, and footwear factories and a wool-washing plant.

Attraction of the City

1.Karakhan Mausoleum

On a hill overlooking the whole city, there is the mausoleum of Karakhan, included in the list of protected monuments of Kazakhstan. Ermek Jolanov writes that Taraz and the region contain most of the artifacts significant for the state, and the mausoleum of Karakhan is one of them. According to legend, it was erected over the grave of the founder of the Karakhanid dynasty, who was the first of the Turks to convert to Islam

2.Aisha-Bibi Mausoleum

This is a monument of the XII century of the Karakhanid era. Legend tells that the mausoleum of fabulous beauty was erected by Karakhan over the grave of Aisha's fiancee, who died on the way to him. The small structure has a cubic base, massive columns at the corners, niches, vaults, pointed arches. The mausoleum is lined with terracotta tiles

3.Zhambyl Regional Museum of Local History

The museum in Taraz has existed for more than 80 years. Here they get acquainted with the history of the region before our era, the period of the Kazakh Khanate and the Dzungarian wars. Now it is a whole complex on different sites and premises, which in general occupy more than 2 thousand m2. For the two thousandth anniversary of the city, the museum has opened new halls dedicated to ancient Turkic writing and stone sculptures, the history of the city, as well as an art museum
